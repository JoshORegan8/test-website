Budapest Municipal Government Imposes 100 Euro Festival Tax on Foreigners Attending Sziget Festival

Budapest event festival Hungary Sziget Festival

Photo: FB

The highly controversial move is expected to be popular with locals

The Sziget Festival, held annually on the island of Ã“budai-sziget in Budapest, is one of Europe's largest music festivals, attracting thousands of visitors from around the world. However, this year, the municipal government of Budapest has announced that it will impose a new 100 euro festival tax for foreigners attending the event.

According to the government, the new tax is aimed at supporting local infrastructure and services that are needed to host such a large-scale event. The tax is expected to generate approximately 1.5 million euros in revenue, which will be used to cover the costs of security, waste management, and transportation.

While the government claims that the new tax will not have a significant impact on festival-goers, many have criticized the move as unfair and discriminatory. Some argue that the tax unfairly targets foreigners, who are already paying for their travel and accommodations, and that it will discourage people from attending the festival in the future.

Moreover, some have raised concerns about the transparency and accountability of the tax, arguing that there is no clear indication of how the revenue generated will be spent or what kind of services will be provided. Critics also point out that the tax may be used as an excuse to increase ticket prices, which could have a negative impact on attendance.

The organizers of the Sziget Festival have expressed their disappointment with the new tax, stating that it will have a negative impact on the festival's international reputation and could discourage future partnerships and collaborations. They have also argued that the tax is unnecessary, given that the festival already pays for all necessary infrastructure and services.

In response to the controversy, some have called for a boycott of the festival, while others have suggested alternative ways of supporting local infrastructure, such as donating directly to local organizations or volunteering.

In addition to the music programme, the Sziget Festival main organiser revealed some other important details. He announced the return of Art of Freedom, which aims to bring thought-provoking and fascinating artwork to the festival.
